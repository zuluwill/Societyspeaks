Our Methodology

How we create the Daily Sense-Making Brief

1 Story Selection

We don't try to cover everything. Instead, we select 3-5 stories that matter for civic sense-making based on:

  • • Civic importance - Does this affect public policy, rights, or institutions?
  • • Source diversity - Is it being covered by outlets across perspectives?
  • • Topic diversity - We avoid overloading any single category

2 Coverage Analysis

For each story, we show you which outlets are covering it and from what perspective:

Example coverage distribution
Left-leaning Center Right-leaning

Source ratings come from AllSides and our own manual classifications. We show the balance, not to say who's "right," but to help you see the full picture.

3 Verification Links

Where possible, we link to primary sources: government documents, academic studies, official statements. These let you verify claims directly rather than relying on any single outlet's interpretation.

4 Editorial Review

Every brief is reviewed by a human editor before publication. We check for accuracy, tone, and balance. If something isn't right, we hold the brief rather than publish something misleading.

What We Don't Do

  • Sensationalize headlines for clicks
  • Tell you what to think
  • Pretend to be objective (we show you the perspectives instead)
  • Cover everything (we focus on what matters for civic sense-making)
