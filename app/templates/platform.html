How it works

Three parts. One rigorous system.

{# Native Discussions #}

Native Discussions

Each discussion presents clear, balanced statements on a real issue. Participants vote agree, disagree, or unsure — and can add evidence or reasoning. No shouting. No comment threads. Structured deliberation.

  • AI-generated or manually crafted seed statements
  • Threaded responses with evidence linking
  • Real-time voting — no page refresh
  • Public, unlisted, invite-only, or private
{# Consensus Analysis #}

Consensus Analysis

Once enough people have voted, machine learning clusters participants by their actual voting patterns — not by who they are. This reveals what polls never show: how views genuinely form and overlap.

Consensus statements

Broad agreement across all groups

Bridge statements

Ideas that unite people who otherwise disagree

Genuine divisions

Real fault lines worth understanding and addressing

{# Programmes #}

Programmes

A Programme groups many discussions into one structured campaign with shared themes, phases, and participant access controls. Everything stays connected, and you can export it all together.

  • Themes: Housing, Transport, Economy — filter and compare
  • Phases: Listening → Deliberation → Recommendations
  • Cohorts: compare views across regions or groups
  • Export all data as CSV/JSON for further analysis
