Short answer: the new Replit version is directionally better, but it’s not “finished-better” yet. It wins on clarity of action, but it risks losing some of your deeper differentiation unless you merge the two.

Here’s a clear breakdown.

⸻

TL;DR verdict
	•	Original homepage → better at values, legitimacy, and depth
	•	New Replit homepage → better at participation, clarity, and momentum

The best outcome is a hybrid, but if I had to pick one as-is for growth and participation, I would pick the new Replit version.

Now the why.

⸻

What the ORIGINAL homepage does well

1. It feels serious and civic

“Where Conversations Shape Our Future”
“Voices of Democracy”

This gives:
	•	legitimacy
	•	historical grounding
	•	“this matters” energy

For:
	•	policymakers
	•	academics
	•	civic organisations
	•	thoughtful founders

This is strong credibility signalling.

2. It explains why you exist

The original answers:

Why should this platform exist at all?

It positions Society Speaks as:
	•	an antidote to shallow debate
	•	a civic space, not a social product
	•	something closer to democratic infrastructure

This is rare and valuable.

3. It doesn’t feel gimmicky

There’s no sense of “growth hack” or engagement trickery. That matters for trust.

⸻

Where the ORIGINAL homepage falls down

❌ It doesn’t tell me what to do right now

The CTAs are generic:
	•	“Join the conversation”
	•	“Learn more”

There’s no obvious first action.

A new visitor has to think:

“Okay… but what should I actually do?”

Most won’t.

⸻

What the NEW (Replit) homepage does well

1. It has a clear participation hook

“Making Disagreement Useful Again”
“The Daily Question”

This is excellent.

It immediately answers:
	•	What is this?
	•	How do I try it?
	•	How long will it take?

That is exactly what Wordle nailed.

2. It introduces a ritual

The Daily Question section is a big improvement because it:
	•	creates habit potential
	•	lowers commitment
	•	removes fear of “having to debate”

This directly supports your adoption goals.

3. It shows motion, not just mission

The “From the News” section does something important:
	•	shows the platform is alive
	•	connects to real-world events
	•	reframes news as deliberation input

This makes the product feel used, not just idealistic.

⸻

Where the NEW homepage is weaker

❌ It slightly flattens your differentiation

The new page is more action-oriented, but:
	•	It underplays bridge statements
	•	It doesn’t clearly explain how your insight is different from polls
	•	The ML / clustering uniqueness is less explicit

To a smart visitor, it risks sounding like:

“A nicer discussion platform”

Which you are not.

❌ The civic gravitas is a bit lower

It’s more product-y, less “this could inform democracy”.

That’s fine for onboarding, but dangerous if it’s all you show.

⸻

The correct interpretation

This is not:
	•	Old vs New
	•	Values vs Growth

It’s actually:
	•	Orientation page vs Entry page

⸻

What I would do (strong recommendation)

Make the NEW homepage the default

Because:
	•	it converts better
	•	it gives a clear next step
	•	it solves participation friction

But…

Reintroduce 3 things from the original into the new page:
	1.	One strong legitimacy anchor
A single “Voices of Democracy” or “Why this matters” section lower down.
	2.	One clear ‘how we’re different’ paragraph
Explicitly say:
We group people by how they respond, not who they are.
	3.	Explicit bridge language
Use the word bridge once. That’s your magic.

⸻

Final judgement

If your goal is:
	•	participation
	•	habit
	•	real data
	•	momentum

👉 The new Replit homepage is better.

If your goal were:
	•	institutional credibility only
	•	long-term thought leadership

👉 the original wins.

But for where you are right now, clarity beats completeness.
